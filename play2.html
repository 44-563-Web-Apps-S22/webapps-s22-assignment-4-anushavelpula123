And the play continues

PHI.

That is a thing of no consequence. He is rich in virtue, and that is better than wealth. Moreover, his father and I were but one mind in two bodies.

ARI.

Let us speak to your wife, and try to render her favourable to....

CHRY.

It is enough. I accept him for my son-in-law.

ARI.

Yes; but to support your consent, it will not be amiss to have her agree to it also. Let us go....

CHRY.

You are joking? There is no need of this. I answer for my wife, and take the business upon myself.

(_Turning towards_ MARTINE.)

ARI.

But [you have to]....

CHRY.

Leave it to me, I say, and fear nothing. I will go, and prepare her this moment.

ARI.

Let it be so. I will go and see Henriette on the subject, and will return to know....

CHRY.

It is a settled thing, and I will go without delay and talk to my wife about it.
