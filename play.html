Anusha velpula

The Learned Woman

ACT IV.

SCENE I. --ARISTE (_leaving_ CLITANDRE, _and still speaking to him_)

Yes; I will bring you an answer as soon as I can. I will press, insist, do all that should be done. How many things a lover has to say when one would suffice; and how impatient he is for all that he desires! Never....

SCENE II; CHRYSALE, ARISTE
PHI .

Good day to you, brother.

PHI .

And to you also, brother.

PHI .

Do you know what brings me here?

SCENE III.--BÉLISE (_entering softly and listening_), CHRYSALE,ARISTE.
PHI.

Clitandre has chosen me to be his interpreter to you; he has fallen in love with Henriette.

PHI.

What! with my daughter?

(_Turning towards_ MARTINE.)

PHI.

Yes. Clitandre is delighted with her, and you never saw a lover so smitten!

BEL.

(_to_ ARISTE). No, no; you are mistaken. You do not know the story, and the thing is not as you imagine.

SCENE IV.--CHRYSALE, ARISTE.
PHI.

Our sister is decidedly crazy.

PHI.

It grows upon her every day. But let us resume the subject that brings me here. Clitandre asks you to give him Henriette in marriage. Tell me what answer we can make to his love.

PHI.

Do you ask it? I consent to it with all my heart; and I consider his alliance a great honour.

PHI.

You know that he is not wealthy, that....
